Designer Story

Elena Moretti's journey began in 2015 after a decade working in traditional Italian fashion houses. Disillusioned by the industry's environmental impact, she set out to create a brand that honored her country's craftsmanship heritage while pioneering sustainable practices.

Her breakthrough came in 2018 with the development of a revolutionary silk-like fabric made from orange peel waste sourced from Sicily's citrus industry. This innovation earned her the prestigious European Sustainable Fashion Award and established her as a leader in biomaterial development.

Today, Elena's Milan atelier serves as both design studio and materials laboratory, where she continues to push the boundaries of what's possible in sustainable luxury fashion.

True innovation in fashion comes from challenging our relationship with materials. We must question not just what we create, but how and why we create it.

Elena's designs have been featured in Vogue Italia, worn by celebrities on the red carpet, and displayed in the permanent collection at Milan's Museum of Fashion and Design. Despite her growing profile, she maintains a commitment to small-batch production and personal oversight of her supply chain.
